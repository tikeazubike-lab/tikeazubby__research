## The Idea

When a major liquidity pool is swept (price moves through a cluster of stop losses), it triggers a cascade of automated orders that amplifies the price move in the same direction, often beyond what fundamentals justify.

## Why It Matters

Understanding liquidity cascades helps traders anticipate volatile moves and avoid placing stops where they'll be hunted. It also explains why "support" and "resistance" levels often break with explosive volume.

## Details

- **Mechanism:** Large players (smart money) identify clusters of retail stop orders. They push price through these levels to trigger the stops, which become market orders that push price further.
- **On-chain evidence:** Funding rate extremes often precede cascades. High long funding = crowded longs = cascade down.
- **Timeframes:** Most common on 1H-4H charts during London/NY overlap.
- **False cascades:** Sometimes the sweep is reversed quickly (liquidity grab + reversal). Key differentiator: volume and follow-through.
