# 30 — Maps of Content (MOCs) MOC

> **Hub notes that organize topics.** MOCs are the table of contents for a domain or theme.

---

## Purpose

MOCs don't contain knowledge — they **organize** it. They are curated lists of links to atomic notes, literature, and other MOCs around a theme. Think of them as dynamic, living indexes.

---

## How to Create a MOC

1. Notice you have 5+ notes on a similar topic
2. Create a new note: `MOC - Topic Name`
3. Add YAML frontmatter (use [[TEMPLATE-MOC]])
4. Write a brief intro explaining the scope
5. Link to all relevant notes, grouped by sub-topic
6. Add to this master MOC index

## MOC Maintenance

- Review monthly: add new notes, remove broken links
- When a MOC grows beyond 20 links, consider splitting into sub-MOCs
- Every MOC should link to its parent MOC and at least 2 sibling MOCs
