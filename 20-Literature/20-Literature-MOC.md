# 20 — Literature MOC

> **External knowledge, internalized.** Papers, articles, books, reports — processed and connected.

---

## Purpose

Literature notes are your understanding of external sources. They are NOT summaries — they are your synthesis of what matters from a source, written in your own words, connected to your existing knowledge.

---

## Sub-Folders

- `20-Literature/Papers/` — academic papers (PDFs + notes)
- `20-Literature/Articles/` — blog posts, news, long-form
- `20-Literature/Books/` — book notes (one note per book, or per chapter)
- `20-Literature/Reports/` — industry reports, whitepapers, on-chain analysis
- `20-Literature/Crypto-Research/` — Glassnode, Dune, Messari reports
- `20-Literature/PhD-Reading/` — papers for PhD literature review

---

## Processing Pipeline

1. **Save** → Drop raw source into appropriate sub-folder
2. **Read** → Highlight key passages
3. **Note** → Create literature note using [[TEMPLATE-Literature-Note]]
4. **Extract** → Pull atomic ideas into [[10-Atomic-Notes/]]
5. **Link** → Connect to relevant MOCs and existing notes

---

## Zotero Integration

- Use **Zotero** + **Better BibTeX** plugin for reference management
- Export citations to Obsidian via **Zotero Integration** plugin
- Citation key format: `AuthorYear` (e.g., `Nakamoto2008`)
